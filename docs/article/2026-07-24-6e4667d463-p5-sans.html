Oveis Gharan’s chosen field of theoretical computer science centers on understanding algorithms, the mathematical procedures that computers use to accomplish specific tasks. Some researchers seek to map the limits of computation, by identifying problems that are too hard for even the cleverest algorithms. Others aim to push the boundaries of what algorithms can do. Oveis Gharan, an optimist at heart, falls squarely in the latter group.

“I’m on the positive side,” he said. “I like to say things are possible.”

Oveis Gharan is slender and sprightly, with unruly black hair and an irrepressible smile. His enthusiasm for his work is palpable, even infectious. “He’s incredibly fun to work with,” Karlin said. “He’s a believer that you can make progress.”
